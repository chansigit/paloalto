Caveats

  • 2D projection quality is distinct from representation quality. High metric scores in 2D do not imply that the underlying embedding (e.g. latent space) captures biology well. Always evaluate the original high-dimensional representations independently.
  • Metrics are approximations. scIB-style and scGraph-style metrics are heuristics. They may favor certain dataset structures and penalize others in ways that do not reflect ground truth biological relationships.
  • Parameter sensitivity can be dataset-specific. Optimal n_neighbors and min_dist values transfer poorly across datasets. Re-optimize on each new dataset.
  • Stochasticity. UMAP and t-SNE are stochastic algorithms. Results may vary between runs even with the same hyperparameters unless a random seed is fixed.
  • Batch correction and visualization are separate concerns. Optimizing the 2D layout does not perform batch correction. If batches remain entangled in the source embedding, no visualization tuning can resolve that.
  • Overfitting to metrics. Bayesian optimization can overfit to the chosen metric landscape. The recommended settings should be treated as a starting point, not a ground truth.
